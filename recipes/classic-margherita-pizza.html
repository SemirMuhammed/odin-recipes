Classic Margherita Pizza

Description

Margherita Pizza is the epitome of Italian simplicity and elegance. This classic dish features a thin, crisp crust topped with a rich tomato sauce,creamy mozzarella cheese, and fragrant fresh basil leaves. The colors—red, white, and green—pay homage to the Italian flag, and the combination of flavors is both vibrant and comforting.

Legend has it that the pizza was named after Queen Margherita of Savoy, who was so enamored with the dish during a visit to Naples that it was christened in her honor

Today, Margherita Pizza is loved worldwide for its balance of fresh ingredients and straightforward preparation, making it a favorite for both weeknight dinners and special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb. pizza dough (store-bought or homemade)
  • 2 Tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tsp minced garlic (about 2 cloves)
  • 1 2/3 cups canned San Marzano tomatoes (crushed by hand)
  • 2 pinches salt
  • 8 oz. fresh mozzarella, chopped or sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves (whole or chopped)

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 550°F (or as high as it will go) and place a pizza stone inside to heat for about 1 hour.
  2. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add garlic and sauté until lightly golden.
  3. Add the crushed tomatoes and salt, simmering for 15–20 minutes to make the sauce.
  4. Divide the dough into two portions and stretch each into an oval or round shape.
  5. Place the dough on a floured pizza peel. Spread about 1/2 cup of tomato sauce over each crust.
  6. Sprinkle mozzarella evenly over the sauce.
  7. Slide the pizza onto the hot pizza stone and bake until the crust is golden and the cheese is melted (about 7–10 minutes).
  8. Remove from the oven and top with fresh basil leaves.
  9. Slice and serve hot!
